History

Nairobi, the capital of Kenya, originated in the late 1890s as a colonial railway settlement. It was named after a water hole known to the Maasai people as Enkare Nairobi, meaning "Cold Water"1. The city became the capital of the British East Africa Protectorate in 1905 and continued to grow as a trading center. Nairobi was declared a municipality in 1919 and achieved city status in 1954. Economy

Nairobi is the principal industrial and economic hub of Kenya. It hosts the headquarters of many multinational companies and organizations, including the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) and the United Nations Office at Nairobi (UNON). The city’s economy is diverse, with key sectors including finance, real estate, manufacturing, and tourism. Nairobi is also known as the “Safari Capital of the World,” attracting numerous tourists who visit its national parks and wildlife reserves

Culture and Lifestyle

Nairobi is a vibrant city with a rich cultural heritage. It boasts a diverse culinary scene, bustling markets, and a thriving arts and music industry. The city is home to several museums, theaters, and cultural centers, such as the Nairobi National Museum and the Kenya National Theatre. Nairobi’s moderate climate and green spaces, like Uhuru Park and Karura Forest, make it an attractive place for outdoor activities. The city’s nickname, “The Green City in the Sun,” reflects its lush environment and sunny weather.
